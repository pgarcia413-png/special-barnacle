How Much Does a Bookkeeper Cost? A Clear Breakdown for Small Businesses

Fri, Dec 26, 2025

Read in 2 minutes

If you’re a business owner, chances are you’ve asked this question while already feeling a little behind: How much does a bookkeeper cost, and what am I actually paying for?

The honest answer is: it depends, but not in a mysterious way. Let’s break it down clearly so you can make a confident decision.

Typical bookkeeping costs

Most small businesses pay for bookkeeping in one of three ways:

Hourly pricing

Bookkeepers may charge anywhere from $45–$80 per hour, depending on experience and location. This model is common for cleanup work or short-term projects.

Monthly flat fees

Ongoing bookkeeping may also be priced monthly, often between $200–$800 per month for small businesses. The price depends on:

  • Number of transactions
  • Payroll complexity
  • Number of accounts
  • Whether reports are included

Cleanup or catch-up projects

If your books are behind, cleanup work may cost $500–$3,000+, depending on how far back things go.

What affects the cost?

Bookkeeping pricing isn’t arbitrary. Costs increase when:

  • Transactions are high or inconsistent
  • Accounts aren’t reconciled regularly
  • Payroll or sales tax is involved
  • Multiple bank or credit card accounts are used
  • Prior records are incomplete or inaccurate

The cleaner your systems, the lower your cost over time.

Is bookkeeping worth the cost?

For most businesses, bookkeeping pays for itself by:

  • Preventing tax-time surprises
  • Catching errors early
  • Giving you clarity on cash flow
  • Saving hours of stress each month

Many business owners discover they were losing more money from poor visibility than the cost of hiring a professional.

A note on cheap bookkeeping

Extremely low prices can sometimes mean:

  • Infrequent reconciliations
  • No financial review
  • Minimal accountability

Bookkeeping isn’t just data entry—it’s about accuracy and trust.

Bottom line: Expect to invest a few hundred dollars per month for peace of mind, clarity, and clean financials. That’s often far less than the cost of mistakes.
